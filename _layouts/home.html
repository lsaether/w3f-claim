This site includes a step-by-step guide to connect your dot allocation, as denoted on Ethereum, with a native Polkadot address. You will also be able to verify that your claim was successful and check the balance of your Polkadot address once the claim is complete. This site is only for holders of dot allocation indicator tokens.

Step 3

Claim Your Dots

If you participated in a previous dot sale and received your dot allocation indicator tokens, you can now claim your dots, i.e. connect the address where your dot indicators have been stored on Ethereum with a native Polkadot address. The claims process will ensure that your dot allocation is denoted in Polkadot's genesis block.

Make sure to use the Ethereum key you used for your dot allocation indicator tokens during this process. You can check your Ethereum key in Step 1.

If you donâ€™t have a Polkadot address, you will need to generate one. You can generate a Polkadot address in Step 2.

When you make a claim, you will be assigned an index as well. This index is a short code -- 6 characters or less -- that can be used as short-hand version of your Polkadot address. Indices are assigned on a first-come, first-served basis.
