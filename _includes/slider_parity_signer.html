Parity Signer (Air-Gapped)

For more detailed information on Parity Signer, please see https://wiki.parity.io/Parity-Signer-Mobile-App

This is the recommended, and if done correctly, the most secure, way of generating a Polkadot address. This will allow you to use a separate, air-gapped mobile device as your only way of interacting with your account.

Step 1

Install Parity Signer app

Obtain an iOS or Android device. Note that this device will need to be used solely for this purpose. That is, you will “convert” your device into a storage mechanism for interacting with your DOTs. It will not be usable for other functionality (e.g. browsing the Internet, making phone calls, running other apps, etc.)

Perform a full factory reset on your device.

Ensure that you set a strong password that cannot be easily guessed. It is recommended that you do not use biometric security such as keypress access or facial recognition.

If this is an iOS device or a newer Android device, full-disk encryption should already be on by default. If it is an older Android device, turn on full-disk encryption. Note that this may take a long time (an hour or more) and turning the system off midway through will cause you to lose all data. The instructions for turning on full-disk encryption here: https://source.android.com/security/encryption/full-disk

Connect to the Apple Store or Google Play store and search for the Parity Signer app.

Click the appropriate button to install.

Step 2

Turn on Airplane Mode and Turn off Wifi and Bluetooth

After the application is installed, you should then turn on Airplane Mode and turn off all wireless connectivity options (generally, wifi and Bluetooth). This will minimize the “attack surface” of the phone - that is, it will reduce the number of ways that an attacker could gain access to your phone. Eliminating network connectivity means that an attacker would need to have physical access to your phone in order to access your key.

Note that although Parity Signer will work with network connectivity, you will see a message indicating that you are “Not Secure”. You can use Parity Signer without turning off network connectivity, but is not recommended as it is then possible for others to potentially remotely access the phone and obtain your seed.

After this, it is recommended that you use this phone only for accessing your account. It will be able to unlock your account by signing transactions without ever exposing your seed to a device connected to a network. All communication will be done through a very limited communication pathway (displaying and scanning QR codes).

Step 6

Store the Seed Somewhere Safe

This may sound repetitive, but it is important to realize that your seed is the key to your entire account. Someone who knows your seed has uncontested control over your account.

On the other hand, if you lose your seed, and your mobile device fails, you will have no way of accessing your account. The Web3 Foundation will not be able to override this - any DOTs in that account will not be accessible to you until you find the seed.

Thus, it is imperative to store the seed somewhere safe, secret, and secure. If your device fails for some reason, you can simply re-enter your seed on a new device after following the setup instructions here, and have access to your account once again.

For maximum security, the seed should be written down on paper or another non-digital device and stored in a safe place. You may also want to protect your seed from physical damage, as well (e.g. by storing in a sealed plastic bag to prevent water damage, storing it in a fireproof safe, etc.) It is recommended that you store multiple copies of the seed in geographically separate locations (e.g., one in your home safe and one in a safety deposit box at your bank).

You should definitely not store your seed on any kind of computer which has or may have access to the Internet in the future.

When you have finished, click on “Backup Done”.

You will have to verify that you have written down your seed before moving on. Remember, if you have not written down your seed, and your device breaks or is otherwise unavailable, you will not able to access your DOTs or do anything from this account.

Step 7

Choose A PIN

You will now have to select a Personal Identification Number (PIN) which will act as your “password” for this account. This prevents somebody who gains hold of your device to use your account unless they know the PIN as well. Be sure that your PIN is secure (not easy to guess for others) but that you can remember it easily.

You will need to enter your PIN for any transactions done with this account.

You will need to enter the PIN twice to ensure that there are no typos. When you have entered the PIN in both input boxes, click “Done”.
