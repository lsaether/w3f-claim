Step 2

Generate a Polkadot Address

There are several ways to generate a Polkadot address

Polkadot{.js} Browser Plugin

If you do not have a cell phone that you use as an air-gapped device, you can use the Polkadot plug-in. This will allow you to generate addresses without a separate device. With respect to security, the plug-in is less desirable than the Parity Signer app.

Subkey

Using subkey for generating addresses is for technically advanced users. This method is not recommended unless you have specific needs and are comfortable interacting with the command line and compiling Rust code.

polkadot.js

Using the polkadot.js user interface without the plugin is not recommended. It is the least secure way of generating an account. It is listed here only if none of the previous options are feasible for your situation.

Coinbase

If you have (or want to get) a Coinbase account, you can claim your DOTs through their system. Coinbase requires at least 1,000 (one thousand) DOTs in your account to use this service.

Parity Signer

Parity Signer is a secure way of storing your DOTs on an air-gapped device.
It will be available after the launch of Polkadot.

If you have any problems, please ask for help in the Polkadot Address Generation Help channel on the Web3 Foundation’s Riot Server or email help@web3.foundation.

What Will Be Generated

A key concept to understand is that you are actually generating two “keys” (strings of characters), one public (the address) and one private (your seed). A useful analogy is a mailroom at the post office. Anybody with your address can send you mail, or put mail in your mailbox, but you need your private key to open your mailbox and move your mail. Similarly, anybody with your Polkadot address can send you dots, but to do anything with those dots in your account, you will need to have your seed (or an application which have already entered it).

Your Polkadot address can be shared publicly, but you should never reveal your seed. If somebody has your address, they could send you dots or associate your real-world identity with on-chain actions. However, this information would not allow them to harm your account or do anything in your name.

If somebody has access to your seed, they have complete control over your account! This means that they can do anything that you could do, including transfer all of your dots to a different account. Neither Web3 Foundation nor anyone else will be able to recover your dots in such a situation.

Bearing that in mind, we recommended that you do not store your seed anywhere on a digital device which can be connected to a network. It should be written down on paper and only entered when necessary. It should never be shared with anybody - again, if anyone has access to your seed, they will have complete control over your account.
